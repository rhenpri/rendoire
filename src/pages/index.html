05 / Reading List

Books I Enjoy

O'Reilly Media

AI Engineering

Deep practical strategies for building modern LLM-driven applications and maintaining intelligent systems natively.

Cal Newport

Deep Work

The definitive guide on cultivating extreme focus to consistently push technical boundaries and generate meaningful output.

Walter Isaacson

The Innovators

A brilliant, sweeping history behind the pioneers, hackers, and creators who built the computer and shaped the internet.

Walter Isaacson

Steve Jobs

The authorised biography of Apple's co-founder. Honest about the contradictions: visionary product taste paired with cruelty, and his obsession with the intersection of liberal arts and technology.

John Markoff

What the Dormouse Said

A history arguing that personal computing emerged not from corporate labs but from 1960s Bay Area counterculture. Frames the PC as a tool of consciousness expansion and decentralisation.

John Ousterhout

A Philosophy of Software Design

A short, opinionated book arguing that complexity is the single most important problem in software. Introduces the idea of "deep modules" and contrasts strategic versus tactical programming.

Walter Isaacson

Benjamin Franklin

Biography of the printer, scientist, inventor, and founding father. Portrays him as the original polymath who balanced commerce, science, and civic life through extreme American pragmatism.

Donella Meadows

Thinking in Systems

A primer on systems thinking explaining stocks, flows, and feedback loops. Catalogues common system "traps" and closes with a famous ranked list of leverage points to intervene in a system.

Steven Pressfield

The War of Art

A short manifesto on creative work that names the universal enemy as "Resistance" — the internal force of procrastination and self-doubt that stops people from doing the work that matters to them.
